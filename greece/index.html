Corfu (Kerkyra)

Corfu is one of the 7 main Ionian islands off of Greece’s western coastline and the 6th largest Greek island overall. The island has many rocky mountains and produces 3% of the world’s olive oil but is perhaps best known for its coastal resorts and having over 100 beaches. Corfu Town, the island’s capital, has a rich cultural heritage after being under Venetian, French and British rule up until it was united with Greece in 1864. Interestingly, at the closest point, Corfu is only 2 miles away from Albania.

Travel tips

There tends to be less people around if you visit early or late in the summer season – and the weather is still great!

Hire a boat to explore the coastline and find hidden beaches.

Things to see

Agni : Peaceful bay with tavernas on the beach & jetties to moor a boot / jump in the sea.

Yaliskari Bay : Remote beach in the next bay along from Agni. There’s a path on the headland that takes you here from Agni beach.

Corfu Town : …is the island’s capital. It has lots of vivid Venetian architecture, most notably two large fortresses built to withstand Ottoman sieges. Corfu Town is also known for its Old Town, which is full of cobbled streets and bustling tavernas.

Kassiopi : A charismatic coastal village in the northeast of the island. It has some beautiful beaches, seafront restaurants and a hilltop castle.

Pontikonisi & Vlacherna Monastery : These two monasteries are unique as they’re both situated in the middle of a bay. Pontikonisi is only accessible by boat but Vlacherna, a symbol of Corfu, is connected to the mainland by a stone pier.

Plane Spotting : The bay that holds the two monasteries is right next to Corfu airport. There are several terraces on the headland where you can watch planes come in to land.

Crete (Kriti)

Crete is the largest & most populated of all Greek islands. In fact, it’s actually 2x the size of the second largest & 5x the size of the 3rd largest. As you’d expect, the island has many wonderful resorts and beaches – even some with pink sand! Cretan history goes back to ancient times and the time of the Minoans, Europe’s first advanced civilisation, who thrived on the island from 2000 BC to 1500 BC. It’s also said that Zeus, the King of the Greek gods, was born in Crete and that the Cretan air has healing powers.

Travel tips

Be prepared for the heat! Specifically, the south of the island is said to have a North African climate.

Things to see

Aptera : An ancient hilltop archaeological site with Minoan & Roman ruins, including that of a theatre. In Minoan times it was the most powerful city in western Crete, strategically placed between two major ports.

Almyrida : Popular coastal resort with a long beach and strip, under 40 mins away from Chania airport by car.

Secret Beaches : There are many hidden bays and quiet beaches along the Cretan coastline. Explore in a car and see what you find!

Pista Park Go Karting : Good quality outdoor go karting track near Chania. Pista Park is the largest in Crete with 12 and bends over 1.1km of track.
https://www.pistapark.gr/

Limnoupolis Water Park : One of the best water parks I’ve been to! It has 11 slides, a large pool, diving boards, a lazy river, a zipwire & more. It’s located near Chania, next door to the go kart track. See here for more info.

Stars : In Crete, the weather tends to be very nice and so the sky is often clear at night. This, combined with the lack of light pollution, means that it’s possible to see lots of stars and constellations! Drive out of developed areas for the best viewing.

Elafonisi : …is a small islet off of Crete’s western coast with pink & white sand beaches. It’s close to the mainland and you can walk through the shallow waters to get there. Approx. 1.5 hours from Chania, by car.

Knossos : Located near Heraklion, Knossos is Crete’s largest archeological site from the Bronze Age and has been called ‘Europe’s oldest city’. With ruins of large palaces and tombs, the city was the centre of Minoan civilisation and culture. It’s said that Knossos flourished for 2000 years! See here for more info.
