Dubrovnik

Dubrovnik, located in southern Croatia, is well known for its unspoilt medieval old town. The city is full of history and has several beaches in the surrounding area. Dubrovnik was also the main filming location for ‘King’s Landing’ in the popular TV series Game of Thrones.

Game of Thrones filming locations

Travel tips

Visit the old town in the morning to beat the crowds and heat.

Consider buying the Dubrovnik Card to save money on the major attractions and public transport.

dubrovnikcard.com

Take a day trip to Mljet National Park – 1 hour each way on a ferry.

Thing to see & do

Dubrovnik Old Town : A UNESCO World Heritage site and perhaps the most well known place in Croatia, with countless things to see.

City Walls : Walk around the walls surrounding the old town – almost all of it is accessible to the public. Great views in every direction.

Old Town Alleys : Stroll along the base of the north walls for some rustic views of the old town.

West Harbour : This harbour/beach between the old town & Fort Lovrijenac is famous for its appearances in Game of Thrones as ‘Blackwater Bay’.

Fort Lovrijenac : 11th century fortress built upon a rock, 37m above sea level. Used as ‘The Red Keep’ in Game of Thrones.

KorÄula

The island of KorÄula is roughly halfway between the cities of Split and Dubrovnik. Much like Dubrovnik, it has an excellent medieval old town (“Little Dubrovnik”) with a great atmosphere and lots of things to see.

Travel tips

KorÄula is a good place to stay for a few nights to break up the journey between Split and Dubrovnik – it’s approximately 2-3 hours to/from each.

Things to see & do

The Waterfront : Quiet restaurants & bars, a harbour and excellent views of the old town (especially at sunset).

KorÄula Old Town : Much like a smaller & quieter version of Dubrovnik’s Old Town.

Cathedral of Saint Mark : This cathedral is a central point of the Old Town and can be seen from every direction. Pay a small price to climb to the top and get the best views of the surrounding area.

Massimo : A cocktail bar built inside and on top of a fortification, with good views of the old town and the sea.

Cats : The Croatian islands are home to many friendly (and some unfriendly) wild cats.

Krka National Park

Famous for its waterfalls, Krka is a national park in the Dalmatia region of southern Croatia. The park encloses most of the river Krka, from which it gets its name from.

np-krka.hr

Travel tips

Visit on a day trip from Split – there are plenty of tour companies to choose from and it takes about 1h15 to get there.

Try to get there for when the park opens, to beat the crowds.

Bring your swim kit as it’s possible to swim in some parts of the national park.

Things to see & do

The River Krka : From Å ibenik, take a boat ride along this river into the main part of the national park.

Skradinski Buk : The longest and most impressive waterfall on the River Krka.

Waterfalls & Rapids : Explore the national park on foot to discover many waterfalls and rapids, just meters away from the footpath.

Swimming : Bring your swim kit as it’s possible to swim in the lakes of Krka!

Mljet National Park

Mljet National Park covers the entire northwest of Mljet island. Its beautiful, unspoilt landscape includes forests and clear saltwater lakes.

np-mljet.hr

Travel tips

Visit on a day trip from Dubrovnik – you can get here on a low priced ferry. It takes roughly 1 hour to get to PolaÄe in the national park.

Hire bikes in PolaÄe to explore the national park – this is definitely the best way to get around and see the most.

Things to see & do

Veliko Jezero : ‘Large Lake’ – the biggest of the crystal clear saltwater lakes.

Saint Mary’s Island : A small island in Veliko Jezero with a picturesque Benedictine Monastery & Church. Use your entry ticket to take the boat to the island.

Biking & Hiking : Hire bikes or explore the trails on foot to see some of the beautiful landscape.

Swimming : It’s possible to swim in all parts of the national park!

Soline : A quiet lakeside village in the national park with excellent views, traditional restaurants and shallow waters.

Plitvice Lakes National Park

Arguably Croatia’s most popular national park, Plitvice has 16 lakes and many fantastic waterfalls. The lakes are situated on various levels, in the middle of a forest landscape. The park is also a listed UNESCO World Heritage site.

Travel tips

The weather was very bad on the day I went to Plitvice however, this turned out to be a good thing as there was almost nobody there! So definitely consider visiting the park even if the weather isn’t great.

Arrive as early as possible to avoid the crowds – it gets very busy in the summer months.

There are nice places to stay in the surrounding villages with good transport connections to the park.

Split

Located in the heart of the Dalmatia region, Split is Croatia’s second largest city and the perfect place to start an Adriatic island hopping trip. The city itself has a superb old town and some nice beaches too.

Travel tips

Split is by no means a bad city but your time will be better spent on the beautiful islands nearby.

Go on a day trip to Krka National Park – it’s just over 1 hour away.

Things to see and do

The Old Town : A historical maze of buildings and backstreets.

Diocletian’s Palace : An ancient Roman Emperor’s palace dating back to the 4th century AD! Said to be “one of the best preserved monuments of Roman architecture in the world”.

Green Market : A lively fruit & veg market with a nice local atmosphere and great fruit.

The Harbour : Waterfront with a boardwalk, restaurants, bars and some cool boats.

Saint Domnius Cathedral : An impressive Roman cathedral located inside Diocletian’s Palace. Climb to the top of the bell tower to look over the whole of Split.

Zagreb

Zagreb is Croatia’s capital and largest city. It’s not on the coast and has no beaches, but the city has some interesting architecture in the Upper Town (old town) and lots of history.

Travel tips

Don’t plan to stay here too long; Croatia has much more to offer elsewhere in the country!

Zagreb is a good place to stop off if you are travelling between Vienna/Budapest and Split/Slovenia/Italy.

If you don’t have a car then the night train is a good and easy way to get to Split without spending a day travelling.
Journey time = 8 hours.
