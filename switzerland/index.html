Bern

Bern is the fourth largest city in Switzerland and, unknown to many, it’s also the capital. The city was founded in the 12th century and its medieval Old Town was built on a narrow hill, surrounded by the River Aare on three sides. It’s said that the founder named the city after a bear, which was the first animal he found on a nearby hunt. As a tradition, the city now has an enclosure and park for brown bears!

Travel tips

Bern is a delightful city and a great place to visit if you’re looking for somewhere slightly less ‘mainstream’.

Visit on a day trip if you’re nearby or passing through – many of the attractions are close by each other.

Things to see

Bern Minster : Gothic church with the tallest spire in Switzerland. Climb to the top for magnificent views of the city and the Bernese Oberland!

Rosengarten : ‘Rose Garden’ – Hilltop floral park with excellent views of the old town and River Aare.

Zytglogge : ‘Clock Tower’ – a 13th century medieval tower built in the centre of Bern. It’s renowned for having a large astronomical clock which performs a unique routine on every hour mark. Over the years, Zytglogge has also served as a guard tower, prison, and city gate.

The River Aare : …is the river which bends around Bern. The Aare has a unique turquoise colour from ice and snow melt in the mountains. Locals enjoy swimming in the river during the summer season.

Albert Einstein : The famous Nobel-Prize-winning physicist lived in Bern for 2 years in the early 20th century. It’s possible to visit his former residence, ‘Einsteinhaus’, where he developed his Theory of Relativity. There’s also a statue of him in Rosengarten (pictured).

The Old City : Bern’s old town is a UNESCO World Heritage site with well-preserved medieval architecture. Within its compact layout you’ll encounter Zytglogge, Bern Minster, Renaissance fountains and Swiss government buildings.

Interlaken & the Jungfrau Region

Interlaken is a town surrounded by the Bernese Alps, in between the lakes of Brienz and Thun (Interlaken translates to ‘in between the lakes’). The town is often noted as the gateway to the awe-inspiring Jungfrau Region, which has charming villages, alpine mountains and other natural wonders. Interlaken is a strategic place to stay for anyone wanting to explore the region and/or the rest of the Bernese Oberland.

Travel tips

Refer to the official Jungfrau Region website for info/tips transport, hikes and places of interest. This is useful for both when you’re planning your trip and when you’re there.

Pick up a free Jungfrau region map from your accommodation or one of the info points / train stations. It’s very handy for getting around as it shows the transport routes and hiking trails. See the pdf version here.

There’s so much to see and do in this area so I’d recommend staying for at least 3 full days!

Things to see

Jungfraujoch : …is a saddle between two of the highest mountains in the region, Jungfrau and Mönch (4,000m peaks). At an impressive height of 3,454m above sea level, it’s also the location of Europe’s highest railway station. Ride the cog railway to the top for spectacular views of glaciers and snowy mountains.

Lauterbrunnen : …is a picturesque alpine town in the heart of the Jungfrau region. Located in a valley of the same name, the town is surrounded by tall waterfalls and dramatic cliffs. It’s also a good connecting location for various hikes in Wengen, Mürren and Winteregg.

Mürren : …is a mountain village at the base of the Schilthorn mountain. If you take the cable car from Lauterbrunnen to Grütschalp, you’ll find an excellent easy hike to Mürren via Winteregg. From here you can descend back down into the valley or visit Allmendhubel, Berg or the Schilthorn.

Wengen : On the cliffs above Lauterbrunnen, you’ll find the village of Wengen. The uphill train journey provides magnificent views of the valley and there are several hikes beginning from the village. Alternatively, you can continue via train to Kleine Scheidegg or take the cable car to Männlichen.

Grindelwald : Perhaps the most popular town in the Jungfrau region, Grindelwald is a beautiful place to visit and/or stay. It’s incredibly scenic and a starting place for many of the best hikes in the area.
See here for more info.

First : …is a 2,000m summit near Grindelwald. Taking the cable car to the top opens the door to some fun activities, great hikes and superb views. Activities include the (free) cliff walk, mountain carts, trottibike scooters, a zipwire and a glider!
See here for more info.

Bachalpsee : …is a gorgeous glacial lake with mountain views. From First, you can take an easy hike through alpine meadows to get here (less than 1 hour each way).
See here for more info.

Pfingstegg : …is located on the hillside above Grindelwald, with sweeping views of the valley. You can access it on foot or via a cable car. There are a few relatively easy hikes starting from the top as well as a zipline and toboggan run!
See here for more info.

Männlichen : …is a mountain between Lauterbrunnen and Grindelwald, sitting at 2,230m above sea level. You can access it via cable car from Wengen or Grindelwald. Once at the top, there’s a couple of great hikes; most notably, the Männlichen – Kleine Scheidegg Panorama Trail.
See here for more info.

Kleine Scheidegg : Located at the base of the Eiger, Mönch and Jungfrau, Kleine Scheidegg is a mountain pass with unrivalled views of the regions most famous three peaks. It’s also the starting point for the Jungfraujoch cog railway.

Schynige Platte : …is a 2,000m mountain ridge with panoramic views over Interlaken, Lake Brienz and Lake Thun. It’s reachable by car or train from Wilderswil (which is 5-10 mins away from Interlaken).

Harder Kulm : Positioned above Interlaken and opposite Schynige Platte, Harder Kulm is a spectacular viewing platform with great views of the Eiger, Mönch and Jungfrau. The platform is connected to Interlaken via a steep funicular.

Lake Brienz : …is one of the two lakes adjacent to Interlaken. It’s an incredibly serene location and definitely worth visiting whilst in Switzerland! Surrounding the lake are mountains, waterfalls and beautiful villages (e.g. Iseltwald, pictured above).

Giessbachfälle : ‘Giessbach Falls’ is a huge 500m waterfall dropping down to Lake Brienz in 14 stages. It’s a wonderful sight to be seen, so worth a visit if you have time. You can get here on a ferry from Interlaken and Brienz, or take an easy lakeside hike from Iseltwald (1h30).

Lucerne (Luzern)

Lucerne is a city in the centre of Switzerland with well preserved medieval landmarks such as the Kapellbrücke (Chapel Bridge) and Museggmauer (fortified city wall). The city gets its name from the neighbouring lake, Lake Lucerne, one of the largest in the country. The lake and city are surrounded by wonderful scenery and snow-capped mountains.

Travel tips

Lucerne is a delightful city, but there’s not huge amounts too see. I’d recommend spending 1 day in the city and then using the remainder of your time to explore the nearby area.

Things to see

Mount Pilatus : Towering over the city and lake, Mount Pilatus stands at 2,128m above sea level. Take the cable car to the top for stunning views of the Lake Lucerne region and the Alps. There’s a toboggan run and zip line at the Fräkmüntegg station.
See here for more info.

Jesuit Church : Situated on the bank of the River Reuss, the Jesuit Church is one of the stand-out buildings in Lucerne city centre.

Kapellbrücke : The ‘Chapel Bridge’ is Lucerne’s most famous landmark. Built in the 14th century, this ornate medieval bridge is decorated with flowers, a tiled roof and a collection of triangular paintings depicting historical events in Lucerne.

Museggmauer : The Museggmauer is Lucerne’s fortified city wall. The wall and its 9 towers were built in the 14th century to protect the town. Some of towers and parts of the wall are open to the public.

Spreuerbrücke : Similar in design to Kapellbrücke, Spreuer Bridge is another sheltered wooden footbridge over the River Reuss. This bridge is less photographed and less busy than its sister bridge; however, it’s every bit as impressive.

Zermatt

Situated at the base of the Matterhorn, Zermatt is a charismatic mountain resort near the Italian border in southern Switzerland. It’s a year round destination, popular for climbing/hiking in the summer and long ski runs in the winter. The town is a car free zone with many nice restaurants and après-ski bars.

Travel tips

The Matterhorn is an outstanding natural wonder and a must-see while in Switzerland!

Visit the official Zermatt website here for detailed information about the area.

Things to see

Matterhorn : Located on the Italian border, the infamous ‘Toblerone mountain’ stands at an imposing height of 4,478m. The Matterhorn is, without a doubt, the most famous mountain in Switzerland and it’s an incredible sight to see with your own eyes.

Findeln : …is a small mountain hamlet facing the Matterhorn. With the snow-capped Alps in the background and picturesque log cabins in the foreground, Findeln is an excellent spot for photographs. The hamlet is also known for its mountain cuisine, served in several restaurants.

Klein Matterhorn : The ‘Little Matterhorn’ is a 3,883m peak with the highest cable car station in Europe! Located at the top is the Matterhorn Glacier Paradise – a 360 degree viewing platform looking out on 14 glaciers and 38 4,000m peaks, including the highest mountains in France, Italy and Switzerland.
See here for more info.

Gornergrat Railway : Ascending nearly 1,500m, a ride on the world’s first fully electrified cog railway takes you from Zermatt to the Gornergrat summit in just over 30 minutes. At the top, there are panoramic views of the nearby mountains (notably the Matterhorn and Monte Rosa), and you can experience the second-largest glacier in the Alps, the Gorner Glacier.
See here for more info.

Zermatt Village : …is a fantastic resort for skiers, hikers, climbers and alpine enthusiasts. The Matterhorn, towering over Zermatt, can be seen from most places.

The Five Lakes Hike : …is an easy trail that takes you past five picturesque mountain lakes: Stellisee, Grindjisee, Grünsee, Moosjisee, and Leisee. Several of the lakes have reflections of the Matterhorn. The whole route takes under 3 hours.
See here for more info.

Zürich

Founded by the Romans in 15 BC, Zürich is now Switzerland’s largest city and the financial hub of the country. With its lively atmosphere, over 1,200 fountains with drinkable water and an old town which spreads across both sides of the River Limmat, Zürich is frequently recognised as one of the world’s best cities to live in and it’s often mistaken to be the Swiss capital (Bern is the capital)!

Travel tips

Zürich is a good starting/finishing destination for a trip to Switzerland. It’s a pleasant and colourful city with enough to occupy visitors for a day or two.

Things to see

St Peterskirche : St Peter’s Church is one of the most popular landmarks in the city. Its clock face is the largest in all of Europe!

Grossmünster : ‘Grand Minster’ is a rather sinister-looking church, allegedly built on the graves of Zürich’s patron saints. Climb the south tower (Karlsturm) for panoramic views of the city (adults = CHF 5 & students = CHF 2).

Lindenhof Hill : …is a square and garden in the western part of Zürich old town. The hill is also an excellent viewpoint to look over the city and River Limmat.

Kirche Fraumünster : …is a 9th century church with a narrow turquoise tower, built on the opposite side of the river to Grossmünster.

River Limmat & Lake Zürich : The River Limmat, a tributary to the River Aare, flows through the city centre from Lake Zürich. For a unique perspective of the city, go on a river cruise; this will take you from the National Museum and into Lake Zürich (and back again).

Uetliburg : …is a peak (“Zurich’s very own mountain”) overlooking Zürich and its lake. It’s an ideal place to relax away from the city and there are several hiking trails too. You can get here in under 20 minutes on a train from Zürich HB (or drive part way then walk 4km).
See here for more info.
