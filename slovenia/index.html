Ljubljana

Ljubljana (pronounced ‘lyoo-blyah-nuh’) is Slovenia’s capital and one of the greenest cities in Europe. It’s a small but vibrant city with lots to offer its visitors, including a castle, parks, riverside restaurants and bars, markets, and a beautiful Old Town. Ljubljana also has a multitude of architectural delights with ancient remains of Roman Emona and modern creations of distinguished Slovene architect/engineer Jože Plečnik.

Travel tips

Ljubljana is located right in the centre of the Slovenia and given that it’s a relatively small country, everything is nearby! If you’re only visiting Slovenia for a short amount of time, then consider staying in the capital so that you can make the most of its convenient location to go on 1 or 2 day trips.

Everything in the city can be seen by bike or on foot; much of the central area is inaccessible by car. If you don’t have much time then hire bikes to see the sites along the Ljubljanica river and explore the parks.

Things to see

Old Town : Ljubljana’s Old Town, Staro Mesto, is the oldest historical quarter in the city. It occupies a piece of land just below the castle and contains many attractive baroque buildings/townhouses (including the Town Hall and two 17th-century churches).

Ljubljanski Grad : The Castle is arguably the most enticing attraction in the city. It sits upon a hill above the Old Town and boasts great views of Ljubljana from its tower and grounds.
www.ljubljanskigrad.si

Triple Bridge : Iconic bridges connecting the centre of Ljubljana with the Old Town. They were designed by the famous Slovene architect Jože Plečnik.

The Franciscan Church of the Annunciation : This appealing church in the city centre is impossible to miss with its stand-out pink exterior.

The Ljubljanica : …is the river that flows through the centre of Ljubljana, separating the Old Town from the more modern parts of the city. River cruises are a popular activity and a good way to see the sights too. Both banks of the river are lined with markets, riverside cafes, restaurants, and bars; the atmosphere in the evenings is especially good.

Dragon Bridge : Dragons have been a symbol of Ljubljana ever since this popular landmark was constructed after the turn of the 20th century. Each corner of the bridge is guarded by a frightening dragon statue.

Trnovo Bridge & Church : Trnovo is one of Ljubljana’s oldest suburbs. Here you’ll find a Roman wall dating back to AD 15, a picturesque canal, and the Trnovo bridge – another deisgn of architect Jože Plečnik.

Eastern Slovenia

Solčava Region

The Solčava Region (“Slovene Switzerland”) is a hidden gem within this hidden gem of a country and it’s often missed by visitors heading to the country’s popular western highlights. The stunning natural beauty in this former European Destination of Excellence is somewhat unspoilt in comparison. You’ll find this region in the northern part of the country, tucked away in the Kamnik-Savinja Alps. It’s main selling points are high-altitude alpine tourist farms, thick forests, blooming meadows, and three parallel glacial alpine valleys: Robanov Kot, Logarska Dolina, and Matkov Kot.

Travel tips

This region is a bit more tricky to get if you don’t have a car. If you do have one though (and enough time), definitely visit here!

If you want to stay in the Logar Valley itself or at one of the tourist farms, then book your accommodation early as these places are in high demand.

Things to see

Logar Valley : This postcard valley is one of the most spectacular sights in Slovenia and the standout attraction in the Solčava Region. It’s an excellent place for hiking, biking, and other activities.
The following website has some excellent suggestions for things to do in the Logar Valley: www.moonhoneytravel.com/logar-valley-slovenia/.

Robanov Kot Valley : …is another of the three pristine alpine valleys in this region. It’s named after one of Slovenia’s oldest farms, Roban, which has been here for over 800 years! Various hikes to the nearby peaks start from here, and there’s also a nice trail through the valley.

Solčavska Panoramska Cesta : The Solčava Panoramic Road is a circular driving/cycling route which visits various sites of natural and cultural heritage in the Solčava Region. Stops include lookout points, a natural spring (with healing powers?!), and tourists farms which each have their own homemade food specialties. Throughout the entire loop you’ll be spoilt with amazing views of the nearby peaks and valleys of the Kamnik-Savinja Alps.

Slap Rinka : Make your way through the meadows and woods of the Logar Valley and at the end you’ll be welcomed by the 90m-high Rinka Waterfall. The base of the waterfall is a 10 minute walk from the end of the valley road.

Repov Slap : …is a secluded cascading waterfall near the quaint village of Luče (10 minute drive from Solčava). Repov can be accessed via a short thematic trail through the woods; the trail starts at a 250-year-old watermill by the side of the main road.

Penzion Kmečka Hiša Ojstrica : …is an excellent Slovene restaurant in the middle of the Logar Valley. The food is delicious and the views from the terrace make it even better. A three-course meal is only €14 per person! Visit at sunset for the best experience.
www.logarska-ojstrica.si

Klemenča Jama : …is a small clearing situated above the glacial slopes of the Logar Valley, at an altitude of 1208m. Here you’ll find a mountain hut which serves wonderful homemade food. It’s the perfect place to relax and enjoy the alpine scenery. You can reach Klemenča Jama via a 1h15 hiking trail which zigzags uphill through a forest. The trail starts near Dom planincev v Logarski dolini in the Logar Valley.

Northwestern Slovenia

Bled

Lake Bled is the most iconic attraction in the country. If you search for Slovenia on Google, this will be the first photo that appears… and it looks every bit as incredible in person. The lake is best known for its picturesque islet which has been home to a church since the 9th century. The combination of the island, Bled Castle, the Julian Alps, and the exquisite lake make Bled an excellent destination for all visitors with many things to offer, such as: swimming, hiking, biking, water sports, horse riding, canyoning… and much more!

Travel tips

If you only ever visit one place in Slovenia then make this it!

Bled is a good place to stay if you’re looking to explore parts of Triglav National Park. There are various companies offering tours (and activities) which are worth doing if you have time.

Things to see

Bled Castle : Perched on top of a steep cliff, Bled Castle houses an interesting museum and it also has one of the best views of the lake. The castle itself can be seen from afar and it looks particularly magnificent when illuminated at night.

Blejski Otok : Bled Island is very popular among visitors. There are numerous ways to reach the island, including: swimming, gondolas, and rowing boats. Once there, you’ll find a small museum, a café, an impressive 17th century staircase, and the Church of Assumption. Legend has it that the wish you make when ringing the church bell will come true.

Mala Osojnica : If you can handle some uphill walking then this peak is an absolute must. It can be accessed via a steep forested trail which starts from the southwest part of the lake. The route takes about 45 minutes and is especially enjoyable at sunset.

Ojstrica : If you don’t fancy a steep uphill 45 minute walk then Ojstrica is the next best option. It goes without saying that you’ll still need to ascend a hill to reach this viewpoint; however, the route is less strenuous and can be completed in as little as 20-25 minutes.

Water Sports : Two of the best things you can do in Bled are swimming and water sports. Lake Bled is surprisingly warm in the summer; I recommend renting a ‘Stand-up Paddleboard’. Canyoning is also great fun; there are numerous companies who organise this (e.g. 3glav & Mamut).

Boating : Another popular activity on Lake Bled is boating, whether that be renting a rowing boat or paying for a ride on the gondola. If you’re up to it then hiring a rowboat is probably more fun, and you can use it to get yourself to the island. On the other hand, riding a piloted gondola is a more relaxing experience.

Soča Valley

The Soča Valley region is Slovenia’s outdoor activity hub. It’s an excellent destination for hiking and biking, but above all else, enthusiasts visit here for rafting, kayaking, and canyoning on the emerald green River Soča. The region is rich with historical sites from WWI and was under Italian jurisdiction between the world wars. Millions of troops fought here and the greatest battle in a mountain area occurred in this valley. The Soča Valley also has an abundance of geological features ranging from cascading waterfalls to rocky gorges and canyons.

Travel tips

If history interests you then I recommend visiting the charming town of Kobarid and following the 5km-long Kobarid Historical Trail. The Kolovrat Outdoor Museum is also meant to be very good.

You could stay in this area for as long as a week or as little as 2 days… just make sure you do at least one activity on or around the River Soča.

Things to see

Kanal ob Soči : …is a small town in the Goriška Brda (Gorizia Hills) region of Slovenia. You’ll likely pass it on your way to your destination in the Soča Valley. It’s worth stopping here to see the Italianesque old town and the double-arched bridge over the Soča River.

Bovec : …is the adventure capital of the Soča Valley. Alongside Kobarid, this is the best place to stay in the are as it is a good distance away from each of the attractions. Bovec is also the location of many of the adventure-sports companies (e.g. Nature’s Ways & Soca Rider).

Kanin Ski Resort & Prestreljeniško Okno : The Kanin Ski Resort sits above Bovec and can be accessed via cable car. The views of the Julian Alps and Soča Valley from the top station and en route are splendid. There are several hiking trails at the top, most notably that which goes to Prestreljeniško Okno – this is a naturally forming window in the rocks between Slovenia and Italy. The window reveals a fantastic view of the Italian Alps.

Small Soča Gorge : This 100m long gorge is up to 6m deep and only 1m wide in some parts. It’s one of many delightful features created by the Soča River. This gorge is slightly less impressive than Great Soča Gorge but it’s still worth a visit.

Great Soča Gorge : You’ll find this magnificent formation just 5 minutes along the road from the smaller gorge, near the town of Soča. It’s 750m long and up to 15m deep in certain places. The Great Soča Gorge is one of the locations where the Soča River looks best… you’ll be shocked at how clear the emerald water is!

Slap Boka : This is the highest waterfall in Slovenia with a drop of 106m and a second drop of 30m. it’s located 5.5km south of Bovec. There’s a trail to the main viewpoint – it takes about 15 minutes to get here from the road.

Slap Virje : …is a more remote waterfall which is nearer to Bovec than Slap Boka. It’s hidden away within the trees, isolated from busy roads and lots of people; this makes it a rather peaceful location. You can access this waterfall on foot or bike from Bovec, or drive (parking is €5).

Italian Charnel House : A winding road form Kobarid leads to this stop on the Kobarid Historical Trail. It contains the remains of over 7000 Italian soldier who sadly lost their lives fighting on the Soča Front; their names are engraved onto the slabs.

Tonocov Grad : …is an fortified hill with archaeological remains from an ancient settlement. This is the second stop on the Histroical Trail. A 1km walk through the woods will take you here from the Charnel House.

Slap Kozjak : After visiting Tonocov Gard, follow the descending trail which takes you through the remnants of the Italian Defence Line and across the River Soča to the Kozjak Waterfall. It’s located within a secluded Karst cavern and falls 15m into a crystal clear pool.

Napoleon’s Bridge : On the return to Kobarid from Slap Kozjak, you’ll cross over the river using Napoleon’s Bridge. The bridge was used by Napoleon’s troops (hence its name) and it has been destroyed and rebuilt several times since it first appeared over 250 years ago.

Triglav National Park

Triglav National Park is the archetype of Slovenia’s captivating natural elegance. It’s the country’s only national park, yet it covers 4% of the land and is among the oldest and largest in Europe. This realm of rocky mountains, alpine forests, caves, waterfalls, lakes, ravines, and river gorges is located in the northwest of Slovenia, between Austria and Italy. The park is centred around Mount Triglav, which is the highest peak in the Julian Alps (and Slovenia) with its 2864m summit. Triglav, meaning “three heads” in English, is a symbol of Slovenia and its three peaks are featured on the national flag. It’s said that every Slovene should climb Triglav at least once in their life.

Travel tips

If you do not have car then I highly recommend paying to go on a tour from Bled or Kranjska Gora. I suggest using Pr1mo Tours or Mamut.

Things to see

Lake Bohinj : …is Lake Bled’s larger sister lake. It’s relatively unheard of in comparison to Bled but it’s much more authentic, substantially quieter, and, unlike Lake Bled, Bohinj sits within the borders of Triglav NP. The pristine lake is perfect for a relaxing swim on a hot summer’s day. It takes 25 minutes to drive here from Bled.

Slap Savica : …is a waterfall very close by to Lake Bohinj. It’s fed by waters from the Valley of the Triglav Lakes and is rather unique as parts of it are hidden underground. There’s an uphill path which will lead you to the waterfall from the road in about in 25 minutes.

Slap Peričnik : …is one of Slovenia’s most impressive waterfalls. It’s a little more ‘off the beaten track’ than Savica and its located near the small village of Mojstrana, between Bled and Kranjska Gora. Peričnik has two drops, the second of which drops 52m from an overhang. The best part about this waterfall is that you can walk behind it!

Vintgar Gorge : …is possibly the easiest and best half-day trip from Lake Bled. The 1.6km gorge has been carved by the crystal clear waters of the Radovna River over many years. A wooden path runs along the bottom of its vertical walls, from end to end. Vintgar Gorge is the nearest point to entry point to Triglav NP from Bled, and it’s reachable on foot, by bike, or by car.

Slap Šum : After crossing rivers and rapids in the Vintgar Gorge, you’ll be greeted by the 15m-high Šum Waterfall. Šum is one of only three river waterfalls in Slovenia. It’s best viewed and photographed from the bottom.
TIP: aim to get to Vintgar Gorge early in the day to avoid queues on the narrow gorge path.

Vršič Pass : This alpine road consists of exactly 50 hairpin bends and takes you on a 50km journey across the national park from Kranjska Gora to the Soča Valley. Half of this route was built by Russian prisoners of war during WWI and as such it’s called the ‘Russian Road’. On the ascent from Kranjska Gora, there’s a brilliant wooden chapel built in their honour. At the apex of the pass (1611m) I recommend climbing an extra 100m on foot to the top of Mount Vršič for some truly breathtaking views of the Julian Alps.

Zelenci : …is a small nature reserve 5km away from Kranjska Gora. It’s a good place to stop at on the way to Kranjsa Gora, Bled, or the Vršič Pass (depending on which direction you’re heading in). The reserve is an incredibly serene place with its turquoise lake, wildlife, and wooden boardwalk; however, it’s most appealing asset is its unobstructed view of Mount Triglav. Zelenci is known for being home to some rare flora and fauna (e.g. snakes), so keep your eyes pealed!

Pokljuka : …is an alpine plateau located between Bohinj and Bled. It’s a beloved spot for hikers in the summer as it has some fantastic views and is the gateway to many mountain trails. The plateau is also home to some wonderful meadows and traditional farming villages. The following website has lots of info about things to do here: www.moonhoneytravel.com/pokljuka-plateau-slovenia/.

Vodnikov Lookout Point : …is an excellent detour to go on when going to or from Lake Bohinj. The viewpoint offers some splendid views of Bohinj, the valley, and the Julian Alps; plus, it’s easily accessible from the road. Be prepared for several hairpin bends on the way here though!

Other Places of Interest

Škofja Loka : ‘Bishops Meadow’ is well-preserved medieval town located between Bled and Ljubljana. Its Old Town has been a historical monument since 1987 so its worth stopping off here if you’re passing by. Places of interest include the Loka Castle & Museum, and the Capuchin Bridge.

Jamnik : This little village in the hills 20km south of Bled has one of the most amazing views in Slovenia thanks to its photogenic church perched atop a a ridge. The drive to Jamnik is also very scenic and offers panoramic views of the Karawanks and the Kamnik-Savinja Alps.

Radovljica : …is a pretty Slovene town near Bled. It has various historic buildings in its old town centre, lots of colour, and great views of the Alps. Radovljica is a good spot for a walk and some lunch.

Kropa : …is a remote historical mining/forging village between Radovljica and Jamnik. For centuries it has been used to mine iron ore for nails and decorative metalwork. There is an Iron Forging Museum here for those interested.

Southwestern Slovenia

Unsurprisingly, Slovenia’s southwestern region is as wonderful as the rest of the country. This is where the country’s diversity really comes to life, offering its visitors 46km of charming Adriatic coastline, UNESCO-recognised Karst caves, forests with bears, authentic wine regions, Venetian history, and more. The southwest is an ideal place to end a visit to Slovenia.

Travel tips

If you’re visiting the coast then you don’t have to stay in Piran. Accommodation can be expensive here and there’s plenty of other options near Izola and Koper, which are only a short drive away.

Things to see

Postojna Cave : …is among the largest Karst caves in the world, and it’s one of the most frequently visited tourist attractions in Slovenia. This collection of caverns, halls, and passageways is over 2 million years old and 24km long! Visitors are able to access 5km of the cave on a 1.5h guided tour.

Predjama Castle : Nearby to the Postojna Cave sits the magical Predjama Castle. This medieval construction is labelled impregnable thanks to its placement at the entrance of a cavern in the middle of a large cliff. Predjama is the largest cave castle in the world and it has a network of secret tunnels hidden behind it.

Goriška Brda : Gorica Hills is a small wine region near the border with Italy. It resembles a mini Tuscany with its rolling hills topped with quaint villages and churches. I recommend visiting Šmartno, a fortified town built on the remains of a Roman stronghold. Slovenia’s wine growing regions are ranked among the top 3-5% of the best locations in the world, so it’s worth visiting at least one of them!

Lipica : …is a tiny village which has been home to a world-famous stud farm since 1580. The farm is known for being the origin of the white Lipizzaner horses which are used by the acclaimed Spanish Riding School in Vienna. Visitors to the farm can have guided tours, watch training sessions, or ride a horse-drawn carriage. Experienced riders can signup for a 1.5h trail ride too.

Piran : Charismatic Piran is the jewel of Slovenia’s small coastline and also one of the most attractive towns on the entire Adriatic coast. Its Old Town, with architecture resembling that of Venice, is one of the best preserved historical towns anywhere in the Mediterranean. Piran is particularly alluring at sunset and has many seafood restaurants too.

Izola : …is a fishing port situated between Piran and Slovenia’s largest coastal town, Koper. Whilst Izola is less-known compared to Piran, it’s still a very nice place to visit for lunch/dinner with its Venetian architecture and winding old streets.

Hrastovlje : …is a small Karst village that sits about 15km inland from the coast. The village is home to the notable Church of the Holy Trinity which was constructed between the 12th and 14th century. The interior of the stone church is decorated with extraordinary mural paintings including the illustrious ‘Dance of Death’ fresco.
